The Dangers and Pitfalls of Habitual Cycles in the Battle Against Worldly Desire  
In the walk with God we are faced with many obstacles to overcome, he gives us tools to  
overcome these challenges but we must use them and more importantly continue to use them lest we  
fall back to our old ways. Another, more accurate way to put “falling back to our old ways” is  
“succumbing to worldy desire”. Often times when we fall into worldy desire it is borne of an  
unchecked habitual cycle, or program, in our organism.  
These habitual cycles play on loops, I know I am not the only one to have “beaten” an addiction  
only to find myself back in the throes of it after a few months. This occurs due to a habitual cycle being  
suppressed to the point it appears to have been eradicated, but in truth only the first battle(s) had been  
won. To elaborate, think of the mind for a moment as a computer running programs. If you can bring  
yourself to be aware of how you have (intentionally or unintentionally) programmed your mind over  
the years, you begin to see a pattern.  
To start with something simple, lets use a cigarette addiction, this could be coffee, sugar,  
alcohol, any kind of addiction, but I will use cigarettes here. Now If you find yourself addicted to  
cigarettes, chances are at some point you decided it was a good idea to try one. As you made this  
choice you created a program telling the mind it is okay to have a cigarette. As time goes on this  
program is updated with each new experience. Maybe you didn’t even really like the first cigarette but  
running off the program “it is okay to have a cigarette” you continued until it was updated to “I like  
cigarettes”. This goes on until the organism is so polluted and imbalanced that the toxic feeling you  
received from smoking you now receive from not having a smoke. As a result of the body trying to  
adjust to a constant influx of toxins, you have become addicted. A new update, “I need cigarettes”. This  
may continue on for some time, many years for some, until at some point, hopefully, there will be  
something new introduced to the cycle.  
A counter program. It may look like “I need to quit smoking”. This counter program comes  
from the same source, you, however it comes from a you that is aligned with the best interest of the  
organism you have been blessed with being the pilot of, not from a you that has been pressured or  
fooled into error from a lack of centered awareness. This counter program, if repeated diligently every  
time the old “I need to smoke” program comes up, will erode and eventually replace the old program.  
The new program can now be updated, “I am not a smoker”.  
Success! It has been some time, the physical addiction has passed and the old program has been  
eroded, and replaced from “I need to smoke” – “I need to quit”, “I like to smoke” – “I like to breath  
well”, “It is okay to smoke” – “I am not a smoker”. I am aware this may be an oversimplification in  
terms of scope and nuance to the layers of habit “programs” we make for ourselves over the course of a  
serious addiction. However I believe the point I put forth remains undisturbed in its foundations. I hope  
it comes across plainly that we must strip and counter each layer of our habits back to the origin if we  
hope to have any longevity in our success.  
Now, what if some of the old programs are left to linger, perhaps due to a lack of awareness as  
to the origin of them, or “letting up”… a reduced vigor in countering them after the perceived victory?  
After some time it is inevitable that the mind will pop out some of these old programs again and if  
there is a lack of awareness as to what this is, the cycle may begin anew. For example, “it is okay to  
smoke” pops up in the mind after a year of not smoking. The awareness is lacking perhaps due to a lack  
of discipline and so instead of playing the counter program, you don’t recognise this as the old out of  
date program that it is, thus it is allowed to linger. What happens now is the mind takes this as  
acceptance of the program and ramps it up as this is what it is designed to do, “Just like riding a bike”  
comes to mind. Now the “I like smoking” program can be played before even one smoke is consumed,  
maybe just from the smell. Lacking the heightened awareness that can be gained from meditation and  
prayer practices, you may ask for a smoke and just like that the cycle is in full swing again.  
Let me be clear, there is no malice in the mind, it is a powerful tool that must be wielded  
consciously to great affect. Just the same way a car driven correctly can bring your wife to the hospital  
to bring life into the world safely, but driven incorrectly can take some ones life. We too must learn to  
master the controls of our minds through proper input.  
This same process can occur in everything down to the emotional responses programmed into us  
by our parents when we were children. With this I am brought to the importance of Faith in the battle  
against worldy desire, as God is the only one who can supply the most powerful counter programs. And  
coming to him through practices like prayer, meditation, repentance, and gratitude is the only way to  
receive them.  
For those who are not “religious” I am not speaking of religion, I am speaking of a true, spiritual  
connection to the living God. Call it creator, the frequency of Love, or whatever you may, it remains  
that there is one source of all creation (if you don’t share this view, look into the Fibonacci sequence, it  
is quite illuminating for some when it comes to intelligent design) and it is fact that we all have an  
equal right to exist within that Love, as we were made from it. Many people in this time know this to  
be true, it can no longer be looked at as falsehood, if we can bring ourselves to live more akin to how  
Jesus did we will begin to see, for Jesus said, “Greater acts than I you will do for I will have gone to the  
father” (John 14:12) Think of it like this, imagine if every second of your life you were followed by a  
camera crew and the camera crew were also (somehow) able to record your thoughts, at the end of a  
month this footage would be shown to your closest family and friends. How would you act? How close  
a watch would you keep over the content of your mind? Any one of us would soon find ourselves living  
a life more aligned with God. Through these sort of practices even the most devout atheist can report a  
feeling of oneness with God.  
To return to what I was saying, through a constant connection with the living god we can also  
renew these gifts that are given to us through our spiritual practice, and receive tools to maintain them,  
as without the knowledge that these gifts come from a force greater than any one person, we begin to  
take ownership of them and thus begin the process of removing them from our lives. These gifts can  
look like Truth, Guidance, Faith, Love, Wisdom, and can be maintained with tools like Gratitude,  
Meditation, Prayer, and Repentance to name a few. The tools become more effective with use just as a  
tradesman learns to drive a nail in one blow with his hammer after years of practice, and the gifts you  
nourish and sustain with these tools only become more ingrained and available to you.  
In this way we can not just quit our addictions, but use spiritual practice and a connection to the  
living God to receive a new life, a new version of ourselves, that has no desire to partake in these  
activities. Because now we can see the hole inside of us we sought to fill came from a disconnection  
from the one who stitched us together in our mothers womb, the one who loved us first, now and  
forever. And now that it is filled, there is nothing in the world that can compare to it. If you are  
someone who struggles with Faith and addiction, look at the ones who succeed in beating their  
addictions for good. More often than not, they have been reborn :) Ask God for assistance in your  
journey, then go within and using these tools find the place he can provide it to you, and he will. You  
will see.  
Yours in Love and Light, You will find your way.
